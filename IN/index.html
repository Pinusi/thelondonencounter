the london encounter

27.09.2014

HOTEL MAYFAIR

The London Encounter is a cultural event involving exhibitions, presentations and discussions which, each in their own way, are intended to provoke us to ask: what do we truly desire, what are we living for, and how can we best face the challenges of life?

It is a place of dialogue for people of all backgrounds and experiences.

Entrance is free.

programme
talks
Business for the Common Good
Today trust in organisations is at a low point.
The financial crisis and on-going issues in the banking industry suggest the financial sector is driven by greed. We have seen similar accusations against energy companies.
We have had the phone hacking scandal within newspapers and the MPs expenses crisis.
Many large multinational have been accused of trying to avoid paying their “fair share” of tax. It seems as if organisations of all types are only interested in themselves and have lost the trust of the public.
Picking up the themes developed in the exhibition on the financial crisis “The Financial Crisis: A Crisis of Desire?” our panel will discuss whether or not business can really be conducted for the common good.
The Educational Crisis
Is education just about dropping your child off at the school gates? Or do we still have a part to play? Education is not confined to teachers and lecturers at universities and schools.
All of us are educators in one way or another – whether by being parents, having responsibility at work or in an organisation, or just through our friendships. But what does it mean to educate and to be educated? Is education only a matter of going to the best schools, achieving excellent results and participating in extra-curricular activities?

The Financial Crisis: A Crisis of Desire?
This exhibition examines the root of the financial crisis and how it challenges each one of us personally to ask what do we really live for.
To be human is to change and to be perfect is to have changed often.
How often do we hear a public figure admit that they were wrong? We know from personal experience how difficult it can be. But to do so, even to change radically, is a sign of strength not weakness.

This exhibition tells John Henry Newman’s dramatic story, from which it emerges that his restless desire for truth was the driving force of his life. This led him to accept both sacrifices and humiliation, and to give up some deeply-rooted prejudices, searching for the real satisfaction that only truth can give: in his words “ex umbris et imaginibus in veritatem” (out of shadows and illusions into truth).
We can all learn from Newman’s journey, especially because “Newman was a person [continually] converting, a person being transformed, and thus he always remained and became ever more himself”

(Pope Benedict XVI on his visit to the UK in 2010).

organisers

The London Encounter is organised by a group of friends who met through the Catholic Movement, Communion and Liberation.

Our faith makes us want to engage in a dialogue with everyone, of whatever religion, background, political persuasion or tradition, about what it means to be human, what truly fulfils us and how best we can face the challenges of daily life. We believe that only by sharing our experience in a spirit of openness and mutual respect can we build the common good together
